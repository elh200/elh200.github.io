Day 3

This is the perfect day to stock up on souvenirs and take in some more beautiful sites. Take a pictaresque journey up to the top of Andreevsky spusk. The funicular is easily accessible by metro or by walking.

At the top of the hill is the classical Andreevsky spusk, a winding street that goes back down to the base of the hill. The street is often called the "Montmarte" of Ukraine and it is vibrant with galleries and craftspeople selling Ukrainian folkart. There are also sellers of Soviet and Ukrainian memorabilia, you can pick up everything from a WW2 helmet to eggs designed in Ukrainian tradition and sports jerseys. The street also includes notable historical sites such as Mikhail Buglakov's house, Saint Andrew's Church at the top of the hill and other sites.

After making your way down the Spusk, you can take a short walk and visit the Chornobyl Museum.

The museum give a fascinating look at the 1986 Chornobyl disaster and is conveniently located in the vicinity of may restuarants. After visiting the museum you may find yourself in a local Georgian restuarant that offers an authentic an exciting journey into Georgian cuisine.
